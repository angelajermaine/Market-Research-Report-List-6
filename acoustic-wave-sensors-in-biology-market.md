<p><p>Acoustic wave sensors in biology utilize mechanical waves to detect and analyze biological substances. The Quartz Crystal Microbalance (QCM) sensor measures mass changes and molecular interactions on the crystal surface, enabling sensitive detection of biomolecules. Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) sensors, on the other hand, rely on surface waves to assess changes in properties like mass and viscosity, offering high sensitivity and real-time monitoring. Both technologies are pivotal for applications in biosensing, diagnostics, and environmental monitoring in biological research and healthcare.</p></p>
