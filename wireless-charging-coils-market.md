<p><p>The wireless charging coils market is divided into two main applications: receiving coils and transmitter coils. Receiving coils are integrated into devices such as smartphones and wearables, capturing energy from the transmitter coil to charge the battery. Transmitter coils, on the other hand, are embedded in charging pads or stations, generating an electromagnetic field to transmit power wirelessly to the receiving coils. This technology enhances convenience and supports the growing demand for cable-free charging solutions across various consumer electronics.</p></p>
