<p><p>The solar microgrid market is primarily categorized into grid-tied and independent systems. Grid-tied microgrids connect to the central power grid, enabling them to exchange surplus energy and access grid resources during low generation periods. In contrast, independent microgrids operate autonomously, relying on local energy sources such as solar panels and batteries, making them ideal for remote or underserved areas. Both types enhance energy resilience, promote sustainability, and facilitate the integration of renewable energy into existing infrastructures.</p></p>
