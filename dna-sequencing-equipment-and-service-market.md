<p><p>The DNA sequencing equipment and service market encompasses several technologies, categorized into different generations. First-generation sequencing, known for Sanger sequencing, is reliable but time-consuming. Second-generation sequencing, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), offers high-throughput capabilities, enabling extensive genomic analysis. Third-generation sequencing enhances speed and accuracy using single-molecule sequencing techniques, while fourth-generation sequencing is emerging, focusing on real-time data acquisition and efficiency improvements. Each generation plays a crucial role in advancing genomics research, diagnostics, and personalized medicine.</p></p>
