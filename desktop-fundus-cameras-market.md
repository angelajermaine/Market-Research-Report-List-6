<p><p>The desktop fundus cameras market is categorized into two main types: mydriatic and non-mydriatic fundus cameras. Mydriatic fundus cameras require the use of pupil-dilating drops to enhance visibility of the retina and other interior structures, facilitating comprehensive examinations. In contrast, non-mydriatic fundus cameras do not necessitate pupil dilation, allowing for quicker, more comfortable patient assessments. Both types serve crucial roles in diagnosing and monitoring ocular conditions, with varying applications in clinical practices.</p></p>
