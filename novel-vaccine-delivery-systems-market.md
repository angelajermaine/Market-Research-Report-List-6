<p><p>The novel vaccine delivery systems market comprises various types, including intramuscular delivery, subcutaneous delivery, and others. Intramuscular delivery involves injecting vaccines deep into muscle tissue, allowing for rapid absorption and immune response. Subcutaneous delivery administers vaccines into the fatty tissue under the skin, promoting a slower release into the bloodstream. Other delivery methods may include nasal sprays, intradermal injections, or oral vaccines, each offering unique advantages in terms of ease of administration, patient compliance, and effectiveness.</p></p>
