<p><p>The fluorescence microscopy market is primarily segmented into upright and inverted fluorescence microscopy. Upright fluorescence microscopes are designed for observing specimens from above and are ideal for thick samples or multi-layer structures, commonly used in materials science and biological applications. Inverted fluorescence microscopes, on the other hand, allow researchers to view samples from below, making them suitable for live cell imaging and tissue culture, where samples are placed in containers. Both types cater to different research needs and applications.</p></p>
