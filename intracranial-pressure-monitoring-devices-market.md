<p><p>Intracranial Pressure (ICP) Monitoring Devices are crucial for assessing pressure within the skull, with two primary market types: invasive and non-invasive monitoring devices. Invasive devices involve surgical placement, such as intraparenchymal sensors or ventricular catheters, providing accurate and continuous pressure readings but carry risks of infection and complications. Non-invasive devices, including methods like transcranial Doppler and near-infrared spectroscopy, offer safer alternatives with lower risk but may provide less precise measurements. Each type serves distinct clinical needs in neurocritical care.</p></p>
