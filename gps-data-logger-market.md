<p><p>The GPS data logger market is classified into two primary types: passive and active. Passive GPS data loggers record location data at set intervals and store it for later retrieval, making them ideal for applications where real-time tracking is unnecessary. In contrast, active GPS data loggers provide real-time tracking and updates, often utilizing cellular or satellite connections to transmit data instantly. Both types cater to different needs, from fleet management to outdoor adventure tracking, enhancing efficiency and data accuracy.</p></p>
