<p><p>Evolved Gas Analysis (EGA) is a technique used to analyze gases released from materials during thermal processes. The market includes various methods: FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy) detects molecular vibrations, providing qualitative and quantitative analysis of gases. QMS (Quadrupole Mass Spectrometry) identifies gas species based on mass-to-charge ratios. GCMS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry) separates and analyzes complex mixtures of gases. ELIF (Excited Lasing Induced Fluorescence) measures specific gas emissions, offering sensitivity for detecting trace components, making each method valuable in material characterization and quality control.</p></p>
